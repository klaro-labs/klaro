Klaro.

myklaro.app

Klaro turns a bare USDC transfer into a business-grade payment — an issued invoice, a screened settlement, and a verifiable on-chain receipt — on Circle's Arc L1.

The problem

A USDC transfer between two wallets is fast and final, but useless to a real business. It leaves no receipt anyone outside the two parties can verify and no audit trail an accountant can close a year on. It skips the sanctions screening every regulated payment provider runs, and it strands the vendor's value in a stablecoin their suppliers don't accept.

The product — one loop, five steps

01
Invoice
Vendor issues a hosted invoice; RLS-isolated per tenant.
02
Pay from any wallet
Buyer pays in USDC, with CCTP V2 / Gateway cross-chain pay-in.
03
Screened settlement
Operator screens, then settles into escrow on Arc.
04
On-chain receipt
An audit-grade receipt is minted and shareable by hash.
05
Partner cashout
Vendor previews local-currency cashout via LP staking.

Why now

Arc makes USDC the native gas token with sub-second finality, and CCTP V2 plus Circle Gateway turn cross-chain settlement into a primitive. Together they make Circle-native vendor rails — invoicing, screening, receipts, cashout — practical for the first time, rather than bolted onto a chain that was never built for stablecoin payments.
